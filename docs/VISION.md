# HMDMQP-2025 Project Vision

An AR-assisted interactive tour experience for a university robotics lab.

---

## Project Goal

**HMDMQP-2025** aims to create an augmented reality tour of a university robotics lab space, presenting information about the lab in a creative and engaging way that goes beyond traditional signage or guided tours.

### Target Environment
- **Location**: University robotics laboratory
- **Hardware**: Meta Quest (mixed reality / AR passthrough)
- **Audience**: Visitors, prospective students, collaborators, and anyone touring the lab

---

## Core Vision

### Why AR?

Traditional lab tours rely on:
- Static posters and signage
- Verbal explanations from tour guides
- Physical demonstrations (when available)

AR enables:
- **Contextual information** overlaid directly on equipment and spaces
- **Self-paced exploration** where visitors discover content naturally
- **Rich media** including 3D visualizations, animations, and interactive elements
- **Consistent experience** regardless of guide availability

### Design Philosophy

1. **Spatial Storytelling** - Information lives where it's relevant. A waypoint near a robot arm can show its capabilities, history, or current projects.

2. **Discovery Over Lecture** - Visitors explore and find information rather than passively receiving it. The waypoint system encourages movement and curiosity.

3. **Layered Detail** - Quick glances provide essential info; deeper engagement reveals more. Not everyone needs the technical specs.

4. **Augment, Don't Replace** - The physical lab remains the star. AR enhances understanding without distracting from the real equipment and research.

---

## Key Features

| Feature | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Waypoint Navigation** | Guide visitors through the lab space to points of interest |
| **AR Passthrough** | See the real lab with digital overlays |
| **Compass & Minimap** | Orientation aids for spatial awareness |
| **Customizable Markers** | Different colors/icons for categories (robots, workstations, safety, etc.) |
| **Descriptive Content** | Each waypoint can hold text descriptions about what's being shown |

---

## Use Cases

### Lab Tour
A prospective student puts on the headset and sees waypoints highlighting key areas: the 3D printing station, the robot arms, the student workspace, ongoing projects. They walk through at their own pace, learning about each area.

### Open House / Demo Day
Multiple visitors can tour simultaneously without needing multiple guides. Each person gets the full experience.

### Research Showcase
Waypoints can be configured to highlight specific projects, link to papers, or show visualizations of research outcomes.

### Onboarding
New lab members learn the layout, equipment locations, and safety information through an interactive orientation.

---

## Success Criteria

- Visitors can navigate the lab independently using AR guidance
- Information is presented in context, where it's most relevant
- The experience feels engaging rather than overwhelming
- Tour content can be easily updated as the lab evolves

---

## Project Context

This is a **Major Qualifying Project (MQP)** - a capstone project for undergraduate students at Worcester Polytechnic Institute. The project demonstrates the application of XR technologies to solve a real-world communication challenge: making robotics lab spaces more accessible and understandable to visitors.
